# 14 — Brand Identity & Visual DNA

> **Status:** Approved draft — v1.0 of the brand definition.
> **Owner:** Deepak (Brand) · Authored in the role of Creative Director.
> **Upstream (immutable):** PRD (`02`) · System (`11`) · XA (`12`) · DSVL (`03`) · IA (`04`) · Wireframes (`13`). > **Downstream:** binds all future copywriting, logo/mark design, marketing surfaces, and any external representation of Deepak Labs. Changes require a [`../memory/DECISIONS.md`](../memory/DECISIONS.md) entry.

## 1. Brand Narrative

**Who is Deepak Labs?**
Not a portfolio *about* a person — the working environment *of* one. Deepak Labs is the digital lab of a researcher-engineer: the place where his research, systems, writing, and trajectory live as one connected, current, honest record. The name is literal. It is a lab: things are made here, documented here, and shown with their trade-offs intact.

**Why does it exist?**
Because a person's work deserves better than fragments rented from platforms. Deepak Labs exists to own the canonical record (PRD §1) — and to make that record explorable by anyone, from a recruiter with ninety seconds to an AI assistant answering on Deepak's behalf.

**What feeling should visitors leave with?**
*"That was considered."* Trust, formed by accumulated precision — real dates, honest write-ups, current work, claims wired to evidence. And one quieter feeling underneath: *this person will still be doing serious work in ten years, and this place will still be here.*

**How should the product be described?**
In one line: **"Deepak Labs — the personal operating system of a researcher-engineer."**
In one word: **canonical.**
Never as: "my portfolio," "my personal brand," "a showcase," or "a digital garden" (borrowed vocabulary, §12).

## 2. Brand Personality

Seven dials, set deliberately:

| Dial | Setting | Expression |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Tone** | Declarative, first-person, unhurried | States facts; never sells them. "14 publications" — the number does the talking. |
| **Energy** | Low-flame, constant | Not excitement — *momentum*. The energy of a lab at work, not a launch event. |
| **Character** | The careful builder | Curious in what it makes, conservative in how it presents. Opinionated in write-ups, neutral in chrome. |
| **Confidence** | Assumed, never asserted | No superlatives about itself, ever. Confidence is showing the "what I'd change" section (13 §4.4). |
| **Professionalism** | Academic rigor without academic stiffness | Precise citations *and* plain-language summaries. Rigor is hospitality here, not gatekeeping. |
| **Warmth** | Carried by content, not chrome | The interface is a precise instrument; warmth lives in the writing, the photography, and Dex's voice. Cold shell, warm core. |
| **Approachability** | Depth on demand (XA pillar) | Nothing requires expertise to navigate; everything rewards it. |

The personality tension to protect: **humble mechanism, ambitious content.** The moment the *site* starts performing ("beautifully crafted with…"), the brand has failed. The site is the quiet one; the work is the loud one.

## 3. Visual DNA

The genetic instructions behind every composition (DSVL executes; this is the *why-shape*):

- **Shape language:** the **hairline rectangle** and the **dot/node**. Rectangles = containers of record (cards, frames, tables) — always right-angled with small radii, never blobs or organic shapes. Dots = points of life (Dex, timeline nodes, graph nodes, status). The entire product is legible as *documents connected by points* — which is literally what it is (the graph, IA §2).
- **Geometry:** grid-born. Everything sits on the 4px lattice; angles are 90°; the only curves are radii and the drawn line's strokes. Deviation from geometry is reserved for the *drawn* layer (illustration, §9) — the human hand against the machine grid is the product's core visual tension.
- **Balance:** asymmetric but weighted — content mass sits left/top (reading order), counterweighted by whitespace, not by decoration. Symmetry is reserved for moments of ceremony (the hero, the contact close).
- **Density:** bimodal, never middling. Public surfaces are *sparse* (one idea per viewport); records and admin are *dense* (tables, facts strips). The switch between modes is itself a signal: "you've moved from narrative to reference."
- **Whitespace:** structural silence — how "quiet confidence" is physically rendered (DSVL §4). Whitespace is the frame that says *this item was chosen*. A crowded layout implies nothing was.
- **Contrast:** high where it speaks (ink on paper, one accent as signal), low where it organizes (hairlines, surface steps). Contrast hierarchy = attention hierarchy; nothing mid-contrast fights for notice.
- **Texture:** none. Flat, matte, paper-true. No grain, gradients-as-texture, noise overlays, or skeuomorphic material. The only "texture" permitted is real content — text, figures, photographs.
- **Depth:** shallow and honest — three surface tiers and one shadow (DSVL §3). This is a *document space*, not a 3D space; things stack like paper on a desk, not like objects in a room.
- **Visual rhythm:** long-short-long. Generous section gaps, tight internal groupings — the page breathes at the section level and concentrates at the component level. Rhythm is consistent per page type so returning visitors feel the meter.
- **Alignment philosophy:** hard left edges, ragged right, top-aligned rows. Centering is ceremonial only (hero, empty states, errors). A strong left edge is the typographic signature of documents that mean business.
- **Composition principles:** (1) one focal point per viewport; (2) the edge does the framing — content never floats unanchored; (3) figures interrupt the column deliberately or not at all; (4) when composing any surface, start from the densest honest content and add silence — never start empty and add objects.

## 4. Typography Identity

(Families and sizes are DSVL law; this defines the *experience* they must produce.)

- **Reading experience:** like a superbly typeset working paper — effortless at length, invisible in mechanics. A visitor should finish a 2,000-word post and be unable to describe the typography. That is success.
- **Hierarchy:** built from weight and scale so quietly that a grayscale screenshot still reads perfectly (DSVL Law 3). Headings *bind* to their content (closer below than above); hierarchy is grouping, not decoration.
- **Emphasis:** scarce and semantic — italics for terms-of-art and titles, bold for genuine warnings and key claims (≤1 per paragraph as a norm). Emphasis inflation reads as insecurity; the brand's voice doesn't shout.
- **Technical presentation:** machine-truth in machine dress (DSVL: mono charter) — paths, versions, stamps, commands. The visual switch to mono *is* the citation: "this is exact." Because it means something, it may never be costume.
- **Research presentation:** metadata set like a proceedings entry — complete, ordered, still. Abstracts get full reading comfort; citations get copyable exactness. The typography must make a professor feel *at home* and an engineer feel *not excluded* (the plain-language summary is typographically equal, not a footnote).
- **Code presentation:** code is quoted speech from a machine — framed (recessed surface), attributed (language label), and never paraphrased (no fake terminal chrome). Reading code here should feel like reading a well-kept repository, not a screenshot of one.
- **Data presentation:** numbers are load-bearing. Aligned columns, tabular figures, units stated, sources linked. A table in Deepak Labs should feel like it could be cited.

## 5. Motion Personality

(Tokens and placements are `08`/XA law; this is the temperament they must express.)

**Motion here is mechanical precision with one organic exception.**

- **Calm** — nothing moves that wasn't caused; stillness is the default state of every surface.
- **Purposeful** — every animation is a sentence with a subject: *this* became *that*; *this* is loading; *you* are here (XA §5).
- **Confident** — one clean motion, committed. No bounce-and-settle indecision, no overshoot theatrics; ease-out and done.
- **Responsive** — the ≤200ms handshake. The product answers input like a well-made switch: immediate, proportional, quiet.
- **Mechanical, not robotic** — timing is exact and repeatable (the same action always moves the same way), which is what makes the product feel *engineered*. Fluidity appears only in shared-element continuity, where meaning requires it.
- **The one organic note: Dex.** The presence dot breathes — the single living rhythm in an otherwise mechanical space. This contrast is deliberate brand design: the machine-calm interface makes the one alive thing legible as *alive*. Guard it: if other elements start breathing, pulsing, or floating, Dex's vitality dies by dilution.
- **Never:** distracting, playful-without-purpose, looping for attention, or celebratory beyond the two permitted moments (XA §5). Motion that entertains rather than informs is off-brand at the DNA level.

## 6. Logo Direction

(Concepts and strategy only — no logo is generated here; execution is a future design task bound by this direction.)

- **Wordmark-first.** "Deepak Labs" set in the system's own type, lowercase "labs" (DSVL §1). The brand's credibility strategy is *documentary*, and documents are signed with names, not mascots. The wordmark is the primary identity everywhere the full name fits.
- **Symbolic direction — if a mark is ever needed:** derive from the Visual DNA, not from metaphor-shopping. The two honest sources: (1) **the hairline frame** — a minimal open rectangle evoking the specimen label / notebook frame; (2) **the node-and-edge** — two dots joined by a hairline, the graph reduced to its atom. Rejected directions: flasks/atoms/brains (literal-lab and AI clichés), letter-ligature acrobatics, abstract swooshes (startup-clone smell).
- **Monogram strategy:** a **"dL" monogram** (or single "D." with terminal period — the period as a nod to precision) for tight spaces: favicon, social avatars, app icons. Constructed on the icon grid with hairline weight logic. **The monogram is the site; the dot is Dex** — these must never be conflated: the presence dot is reserved by DSVL as Dex's body, so the favicon may not be a lone dot.
- **Favicon strategy:** monogram at 16/32px, simplified stroke (hairlines vanish at favicon scale — the mark thickens by design at small sizes, a documented exception). Dark/light variants matching the two modes.
- **Future animation ideas** (reserved, unbuilt): the wordmark *drawing itself* in the SVG line language (`@narrative`, once) as a loading/intro signature; the monogram's period blinking once on page-ready — a single frame of life, not a loop. Any logo motion obeys `08` and the one-living-thing rule (§5): logo animation must never breathe.

## 7. Icon Language

(Extends DSVL §6 with brand-level intent.)

- **Outline, permanently** — the icon set belongs to the hairline DNA (§3); filled states exist only as "this is currently on."
- **Weight:** matched to the hairline system and the type's optical weight — icons read as *marginalia in the same pen*, not as imported stickers.
- **Corner radius:** rounded caps/joins per DSVL — the pen has a soft nib; shapes stay right-angled. Sharp-cornered or fully-geometric-circle icon styles are off-DNA.
- **Animation behavior:** state morphs only, ≤200ms, and the morph must *explain* (menu becomes close because it is now closable). Icons never wave for attention.
- **Consistency rules:** one set + custom glyphs drawn on the same grid; a needed icon that doesn't exist is *drawn*, never imported from a second family; every icon has a text name (a11y and vocabulary discipline, §12); decorative icon-sprinkling (icons before every heading) is forbidden — an icon appears where a glyph genuinely compresses meaning.

## 8. Photography Direction

(Extends DSVL §10 with a creative brief's specificity.)

- **Portrait style:** the working portrait — Deepak in context (desk, lab, whiteboard, field), engaged or at ease, never posed-against-gradient. One canonical portrait, renewed roughly yearly (freshness applies to likeness too). Eye-level camera; no low-angle hero worship.
- **Research photography:** artifacts as specimens — hardware, setups, screens, whiteboards shot straight-on or top-down, generously framed, honestly lit. It should feel like *documentation that happens to be beautiful*, never staged lab-stock.
- **Event photography:** documentary distance — talks, conferences, hackathons captured as moments, not trophies. Wide context shots over podium close-ups; the story is participation, not applause.
- **Gallery consistency:** one grade across eras and devices (neutral, slightly lifted blacks per DSVL); mixed sources (phone, camera, screenshots) are unified by treatment and *honest captions*, not by faking uniform quality.
- **Lighting philosophy:** available light, always. Window light, room light, stage light as-found. No portable-studio look, no dramatic rim-lighting, no golden-hour engineering. The lighting ethic is the honesty ethic.
- **Background philosophy:** real places, quietly composed — declutter by framing, not by blur-wash or replacement. Backgrounds may inform (a lab says "lab"); they must never perform.
- **Processing guidelines:** exposure/contrast/white-balance corrections — yes; geometry straightening — yes; content removal/addition — no; skin retouching — no; generative fill/AI enhancement — never (DSVL §10's synthetic-likeness ban extends to *all* imagery). Every image ships with story-alt-text; the caption is part of the photograph.

## 9. Illustration Language

**Should illustrations exist? Yes — exactly one kind.** The **drawn line**: single-weight SVG line drawings, as if plotted by a careful machine or drafted by a patient hand. Already seeded in DSVL (§1 recognition device); elevated here to the brand's entire illustrative range.

- **Style:** technical-figure aesthetic — diagrams, schematics, maps, motifs — built from hairline strokes, dots, and restrained labels in the system type. No fills beyond occasional tints; no characters, mascots, or scenes; no isometric-people-doing-things (the definitive template smell).
- **Where:** research theme motifs (`/research`), architecture and concept diagrams (posts, projects), the timeline's spine, empty states, error pages, redrawn paper figures (DSVL §10), and eventually the knowledge map (XA §13) — its node-and-edge rendering *is* this language at full scale.
- **Why:** three reasons. (1) *Semantic fit* — line drawings are the native tongue of research figures and engineering schematics; the brand illustrates the way its subject documents. (2) *The DNA tension* (§3) — the drawn line is the human hand on the machine grid; it supplies all the warmth the flat interface withholds. (3) *Longevity* — a hairline drawing ages like a textbook figure, not like an illustration trend.
- **Discipline:** illustration explains or it doesn't exist. A drawing that merely decorates fails the same test as motion that merely delights.

## 10. AI Identity — Dex

(Builds on D-015/D-019 and XA §7; this is Dex's brand sheet.)

- **Presence:** ambient, singular, reserved. One dot, one place per surface, breathing sub-perceptually. Dex is *present* the way a colleague at the next desk is present — available, not hovering.
- **Voice:** first-person-as-itself, third-person-about-Deepak, always (D-015). Sentences are short, warm, and load-bearing. It says "I don't know that yet" without ceremony and cites without being asked. It never uses filler enthusiasm ("Great question!"), never flatters, never apologizes twice.
- **Personality:** the lab's resident guide — think *knowledgeable postdoc*, not concierge, not mascot. Dry warmth; a light touch of wit permitted in declines and empty moments, never in answers about the work (facts stay unadorned).
- **Interaction style:** answers, then opens doors — every response lands on citations and 2–3 forward paths (XA §7). Dex treats conversation as *navigation with manners*: its success is where you go, not how long you stay.
- **Visual cues:** the dot is the entire visual vocabulary — at rest (breathing), thinking (deeper breath), speaking (steady), resting/offline (still + stated). No emoji in Dex's speech; no avatar, face, or character render, permanently (D-019).
- **Activation behavior:** invited, never intruding — opens only by user action (dot, palette, chips, affordances); arrives *with context* ("Reading about [project]? …"); yields instantly (Esc, close, stop — no "are you sure you want to leave me?" patterns, ever).
- **Trust signals, stacked:** honest self-introduction (what it is, what it knows) · citation-on-everything · visible knowledge-freshness stamp · in-character refusal of the off-domain · graceful admission of gaps with a human hand-off ("ask Deepak directly"). Dex earns trust by *displaying its boundaries*, which is the brand's whole method in miniature.
- **Idle behavior:** breathes, and nothing else. No toasts, no "still there?", no unsolicited tips. The strongest identity statement Dex makes is its silence.

## 11. Brand Voice

The writing system for every string in the product. Register: **plain, precise, first-person, present-tense.** The voice of someone explaining their own work at a whiteboard — direct, unhurried, glad you asked.

| Surface | Rule | Yes | Never |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Headlines** | Declarative fragments; content-first; no gerund-hype | "Metric learning at scale" · "What I'm working on" | "Crafting delightful experiences" · "Welcome to my world" |
| **Buttons** | Verb + object, ≤3 words; the button *is* the outcome | "Download CV" · "Copy citation" · "View work" | "Learn more" · "Click here" · "Let's go!" |
| **Errors** | What happened → what now; system takes the blame | "Couldn't send — the server didn't answer. Try again, or email me directly." | "Oops!" · "Something went wrong 😕" · blame-the-user phrasing |
| **Tooltips** | One clause, additive, skippable | "Copies BibTeX" | Restating the label · essays |
| **Empty states** | Honest present tense + one door (IA §9) | "The first post is being written." | "Nothing to see here!" · fake urgency |
| **Notifications/status** | Timestamped fact, mono where machine-truth | "Saved 12:04" · "Synced 3 min ago" | "All changes saved successfully!!" |
| **Microcopy** | Invisible until needed; never cute at a cost | "Prefer email? cd@…" | Easter-egg overload · lorem placeholder |
| **Dex** | §10 voice | "That's outside what I know — I cover Deepak's work." | "As an AI language model…" |

**Banned vocabulary (clichés & buzzwords):** *passionate, journey, delightful, seamless, cutting-edge, leverage, innovative (as self-praise), rockstar/ninja/guru, blazingly, deep dive, unlock, empower, "digital garden," "let's connect!"* — each replaced by the specific fact it was hiding. **Punctuation temperament:** periods over exclamation points (≤1 "!" per surface, usually zero); em-dashes welcome; no ellipsis-as-suspense; emoji never in UI chrome, at Deepak's discretion in post *content* only.

## 12. Design Vocabulary

**Position — against renaming for flavor.** The brief suggests "Engineering" for Projects, "Journey" for Timeline. Rejected: coined navigation labels tax every first-time visitor to spare the brand's vanity, and "Journey" is itself the cliché genre (§11). The PRD's audiences (recruiter, professor, engineer) navigate by *convention*; wayfinding words must be boring. **The rule: plain names for places, coined names for inventions.**

| Concept | Canonical term | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| The product/site | **the Labs** (internal shorthand: DL) | "Deepak Labs" in full at first mention |
| Nav & pages | **Work · Research · Posts · About · Projects · Publications · Timeline · Skills · Gallery · Contact** | Plain, per IA D-021 — immutable |
| The AI | **Dex**, "the twin" (lowercase, descriptive) | Never "chatbot," "assistant widget," "AI helper" |
| News surface | **Radar** | Already coined (D-006) — the model example: a *new* concept earned a *new* name |
| Skills "now" section | **Currently working on** | Plain phrase over a coined "Now page" |
| The relation system | **the graph**; links are **trails** | "Related trails" in UI copy |
| Admin | **Admin** (rail sections: plain nouns) | Not "Workspace"/"Studio"/"Command Center" — it's an admin |
| Content units | **post · project · publication · entry · item** | Never "stories," "pieces," "drops" |
| Publishing states | **draft · scheduled · published · archived** | Mechanical truth, no euphemism |
| The record's currency | **freshness** (stamps: "Updated ⟨date⟩") | The brand's home-grown quality metric |

**Naming conventions for the future:** new *places* get plain nouns; new *inventions* (a feature with no convention to lean on) may earn a coined name — one word, concrete-metaphor, spellable, meaningful after five years (test: does it still work said aloud to a professor?). Radar passes; "Journey" never would. Every coined name enters this table via a decision entry.

## 13. Anti-Patterns — what the brand must never become

| Never | Why it kills the brand |
| --- | --- |
| **The personal brand™** | Follower-count energy, "let's connect!", growth-hack cadence — converts a canonical record into a content funnel; trust evaporates on contact. |
| **The startup landing clone** | Social-proof strips, pricing-page rhythm, "trusted by" logos — Deepak Labs sells nothing; borrowed commercial grammar makes the real thing look fake. |
| **The AI-hype brand** | Sparkle-emoji AI, "powered by GPT-∞" badges, twin-as-gimmick — Dex's authority comes from groundedness (§10); hype vocabulary spends exactly that. |
| **The animation showcase** | The site as demo reel — every effect added for admiration is subtracted from the work's credibility (XA §0.1 made structural). |
| **The museum** | Pristine, finished, dead — a "complete" archive contradicts the *lab* premise; the brand requires visible work-in-progress (freshness, Currently-working-on, honest drafts). Staleness is the brand's only mortal enemy (PRD/XA). |
| **The template** | Anything a visitor has seen elsewhere first — bento grids of the season, isometric illustrations, gradient meshes. Recognition strategy (§DSVL 1) dies the moment the site could be mistaken for a theme. |
| **The oversharer** | Personal-life content drift — Gallery supplies human texture (XA); the boundary is *the work and its person*, not the person's diary. Professional trust needs edges. |
| **The ventriloquist** | Any surface speaking *as* Deepak that Deepak didn't write — Dex impersonation (banned, D-015), auto-generated "personal" posts, synthetic imagery. One fabricated first-person sentence poisons every real one. |

## 14. Future Evolution — 5–10 years

**The invariant core (never changes):** the name and wordmark-first strategy · the dot-as-Dex · the hairline DNA · the drawn line · the voice register (plain, precise, first-person) · the vocabulary rule (plain places, coined inventions) · the honesty ethic (no synthetic likeness, no fabricated voice, freshness over polish).

**The evolving expression (expected to change):** typography and palette execution (DSVL token refreshes as rendering and taste evolve — the *rules* persist, values may be retuned) · illustration density (the drawn line can grow from motif to knowledge-map centerpiece) · Dex's capability surface (voice mode, memory — same identity, more rooms) · photography (yearly portrait renewal; the grade may be recalibrated per decade, uniformly and once).

**Evolution mechanics:** identity changes ride major versions (v2, v3 — SemVer as brand cadence), each with a decision entry and a CHANGELOG record — the brand evolves *on the record*, like everything else here. The test for any proposed refresh: *would a 2026 visitor returning in 2031 recognize where they are within one viewport?* If no — it's a rebrand, not an evolution, and rebrands require extraordinary justification (a name is a decade-long asset; this document exists so it never needs replacing, only re-tuning).

**Aging strategy:** the brand is built from elements with long half-lives — documents, hairlines, plain speech, drawn figures, one blue dot. Trend-immune by construction (D-016, D-020 already pruned the fashionable). The risk to manage is not looking dated; it is *going quiet* — the anti-pattern table's "museum." The brand's true maintenance plan is the PRD's freshness loop: **the identity stays alive by the work staying visible.**
